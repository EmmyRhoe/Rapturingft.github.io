WATER BAPTISM Baptizo and Baptisma are Greek words translated as baptize and baptism respectively. Baptizo (to baptize) is derived from the Greek word, ‘Bapto' which means to overwhelm, i.e. cover wholly with a fluid; to immerse, dip. Baptize itself means to overwhelm, make fully wet. Baptisma on the other hand denotes baptism consisting of the process of immersion, submersion and emergence and is used (1) of John's baptism, (2) of Christian baptism, (3) of the overwhelming afflictions and judgments to which the Lord voiuntarily submitted on the Cross (Luke 12:50), (4) of the sufferings Christ's followers would experience, not of a vicarious character, but in fellowship with the sufferings of their master (Mark 10:38-39) Baptism is used only in the New Testament for ceremonial ablution especially of the ordinance of Christ The Right Way of Baptism From the above definitions, it is clear that water baptism must be done by immersion otherwise it is not baptism since baptism means immersion or dipping.
